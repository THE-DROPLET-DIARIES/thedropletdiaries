Why Did We Choose our name THE DROPLET DIARIES?

We were recently asked why we would choose “The Droplet Diaries” as a name while knowing the potential to fuel disinformation. This platform and content are intended to untangle the droplet dogma.

Keeping Our Name

We were recently asked why we would choose “The Droplet Diaries” as a name while knowing the potential to fuel disinformation. This platform and content are intended to untangle the droplet disinformation or what we sometimes refer to as droplet dogma. No doubt, the highway to hell is often paved with good intentions. We have a backstory and plan. Oh, but first, there’s something you should know.

As the creator, I have deleted and recreated all content for this website and social media several times because the message has been challenging to capture. The stakes of promoting droplet dogma are high because this virus kills and disables. I keep these words in mind, written by Confucius from “rectifying the names.”

We kept the name, The Droplet Diaries because the word “droplet” was the actual word used to screw people from access to airborne precautions. Our goal is to help people understand the words droplet and aerosol. We can empower individuals to advocate for themselves using the proper terminology, thereby focusing efforts on what remains undone, airborne precautions for all.

A Part of the Backstory

On March 21, 2020, I illustrated my dream here in Image 3. When this was created, the CDC and WHO maintained that COVID spread by “droplet” and “the science” was unclear whether masks would work within the community. At the time, this was the dream, and I would tell the world, “masks protect from diseases spread by spit.” I’ve learned since this that it isn’t entirely true that droplets and aerosols are made out of spit. Check out our page “What’s the Difference between Droplets and Aerosols?” here

I think the world before 2020 knew that masks worked as intended for occupational safety, healthcare workers, wildfires, etc as shown in Image 4. Taking it a step further, I believe that in 2019 most people understood that not all masks work the same.

Broken Dreams

Here’s the thing, thoughâ€“ There were these three weeks in early 2020. Maybe late February to sometime mid-March, either wayâ€” It was a time when America, maybe the world, was magical and full of possibilities. We could have taken on anything together, and Image 5 could’ve been us. The Droplet Diaries was born from the ashes of these possibilities.

America and other parts of the world were naive to believe that masks wouldn’t slow the spread of a respiratory pathogen. They even told us to “leave the masks” for the healthcare workers because masks work.

It was confusing, and we were in transmission limbo for months. Imagine the thoughts people had to rationalize the CDC guidelines. It was wrong to mislead people about masks, even with the logistical challenges of a global mask shortage. We lost our collective common sense and shamed each other to save masks for healthcare workers. It took the CDC one year to inform the public that the risk of surface contamination was low and two years to recommend respirators. We were not waiting on the science. We lost an opportunity to apply science and innovate PPE, such as masks for community settings.

The Truth

The truth is “the science” that did not change. Masks were always going to work and, in some circumstances, require modifications. N95 masks were always going to outperform surgical masks. Why? Because N95 masks are classed as respiratory protection and designed to be superior to surgical masks.

Silencing Silent Spread

This needs to be said. We knew about the possibility of asymptomatic transmission by at least February 2020. Some experts and governments chose to ignore the possibility of asymptomatic transmission of SARS2. This choice left everyone outside of healthcare unprotected and killed the idea of personal responsibility. Healthcare workers were also abandoned by this policy which is a complex topic and additionally involves the discussion of aerosol-generating procedures.

By July 2020, I started this website and joined social media platforms Instagram and Twitter. As mentioned earlier, I have deleted and recreated all of our content and graphics several times because of a commitment to rectifying the names droplet and aerosol. Lest we forget, we need universal airborne precautions to protect against an airborne virus, SARS2. The only way out is through our collective understanding of how this virus spreads.

Once we get the names right, maybe we can also start to separate the difference between droplet and airborne precautions. We are faced with the problem that the protections we have implemented for COVID, target droplets and not aerosols. We’ve been doing it wrong, and that is a tough pill to swallow; however, we are prepared to get the conversation started and highlight the solution. SARS2 is airborne. Our solution relies on clearing the indoor air with layers of ventilation, filtration, UVGI, and wearing an N95 respirator.
